UX/UI Portfolio Design Process

PURPOSE:

Introduce who I am as a creative designer. To create a portfolio website which showcase my design aesthetic, skills and experience.

AUDIENCE:

The user of this website will be other creative design professionals and firms. These professionals will most likely own their own business and be looking for a graphic designer who can build their website, as well as create their marketing and images. Showcases my work as a designer showing my capabilities and vision to hire me.

MESSAGE:

The message received from the user should clearly articulate my design skills are able to complete designs in an organized and effective manner.

CONTEXT:

Having an organized and eye-catching design portfolio can easily turn a chance meeting with a stranger into an interview for a potential client or employer. Introducing myself and getting a background on who I am. Which would include my portfolio, resume, work experience, education background- works page featuring some of my previously done works in categories; logo designs, UX/UI, Photoshop, illustration, technical/desogn skillls, and contact information.

VOICE:

Simplistic professional style design. Consice, informed, professional, contemporary, organize and personable.

RESPONSE:

Be able to find a job opportunities within this field.

PERSONA:

Define Users

￼IN-HOUSE ADVERTISING AGENCY

An in-house agency is owned and operated by its one and only client: the advertiser. Instead of a company outsourcing its advertising and collateral to an agency, its marketing and communications requirements are mainly handled by its own in-house agency. They are looking for a graphic designer who can build their website, as well as create their marketing and images.

￼JR. DESIGNER

A Junior Graphic Designer will lay out pages, draw logos, redraw logos, rework text, perform color corrections and overall take on the basic duties that help them get to know the ins and outs of graphic design. Their knowledgable in all Adobe suites like Ai, Ps, and Id.A junior designer will often work on multiple aspects of one project but will take on only one or two tasks at a time. Graphic Designer will have to be able to clearly communincate with the team to ensure the work is cohesive and met to the highest standard.
